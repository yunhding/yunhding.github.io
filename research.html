Research Description
Spacetime symmetries play a crucial role in understanding Nature's secrets. In particular, invariances under Lorentz transformations (rotations and boosts) and the combined CPT transformation (C for charge conjugation, P for parity inversion, and T for time reversal) are foundations of the two current best theories describing Nature, General Relativity (GR) and the Standard Model (SM). However, it is known that Lorentz and CPT symmetry can be naturally broken in an underlying theory that unifies gravity with quantum physics. Motivated by this, my theoretical research focuses on testing Lorentz and CPT symmetry in precision experiments. More specifically, in the context of Lorentz and CPT violation (see this from Prof. Kostelecký’s webpage for a comprehensive background information on Lorentz and CPT violation), I work in the following directions:
  • Lorentz- and CPT-violating quantum electrodynamics (QED);
  • Particle and antiparticle comparisons in Penning traps;
  • Corrections to particle's spin precession in Electric Dipole Moment (EDM) experiments;
  • Lorentz and CPT tests in atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) systems.
